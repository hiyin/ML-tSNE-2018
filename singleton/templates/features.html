    Features

    Upload

    Singleton accepts raw data of single cell expressions in either HDF5 (Hierarchical Data Format 5) or CSV (Comma Separated Values) file formats. It is assumed that the raw sequence data has already been processed, summarised and normalised. In addition, an option to share with a group is available to gain access the data for collaboration by either invitation through email, or by sharing a link.

    Analyse

    t-distributed Stochastic Neighbour Embedding (t-SNE) clustering is a technique for reducing dimensions to process the uploaded data. It is useful to differentiate heterogeneous populations of genes in a single cell.

    Visualise

    A gene expression heatmap and a multidimensional scaling (MDS) scatterplot are produced from t-SNE clustering. With a heatmap, gene expressions of single cells are easily distinguished. In a scatterplot, a lasso tool is embedded to the D3 library to compare two different clusters. A table of gene expressions are also produced to identify which group a sample belongs to.

    Export

    After the analysis of data, heatmaps and scatterplots can be saved in PNG (Portable Network Graphics) files. Tables generated from the analysis can also be saved in the CSV format. For future reference, the system automatically saves the analysis on the server.
